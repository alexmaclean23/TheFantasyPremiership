ABOUT

The Fantasy Premiership is an ESPN redraft league, split into three eight-team divisions with yearly promotion and relegation. The divisions feature independent player pools, but are commissioned collectively and observe identical rulesets.

Founded in 2023 in Amherst, Massachusetts, The Fantasy Premiership now features owners from across the country, with most years resulting in divisional expansion to accommodate the growing applicant pool.

Membership is applied for yearly, with returning owners receiving priority status. Interested parties are welcome to apply at any point during the offseason, with league membership announced one month prior to the draft.

SEASON

The Fantasy Premiership runs yearly seasons from August to January, drafting prior to NFL Week 01 and concluding the playoffs during NFL Weeks 16-17. The playoffs stretch over the final four weeks of the season, with two rounds occupying two NFL weeks each.

At the season's conclusion, the highest-performing owners receive winnings in accordance with the league buyins, as well as promotion up to the next division. Similarly, the lowest-performing owners will be relegated down to the previous division. Both promotion and relegation may vary depending on an owner's current league, and may be affected by divsion expansion the following season.

1. The League

1.1. Divisions

1.1.1. Structure

The League will be split into eight team divisions, numbered from one. The divisions will operate under identical rulesets, but run individually with independent player pools, standings, and playoff races. Owners are restricted to a single team in a single division, with no joint ownership allowed.

1.1.2. Expansion

The League will handle all potential expansions during the offseason. In order to expand with a new division, the League must receive at least five acceptable applications beyond the capacity of the existing divisions. In these instances, a new division will be created for the additional applicants and filled with automanaged teams for the remaining vacancies.

1.2. Transfers

1.2.1. Promotion

The League will promote the top two teams from the final standings of each division, excluding the first division, at the end of every season. The promoted teams will be subject to the buyin, payout, rewards, and punishments of their new division the following season.

1.2.2. Relegation

The League will relegate the bottom two teams from the final standings of each division, excluding the last division, at the end of every season. The relegated teams will be subject to the buyin, payout, rewards, and punishments of their new division the following season.

1.3. Transactions

1.3.1. Buyins

The League buyin varies by division, with higher divisions charging higher buyins and vice versa. Buyins are calculated by using 100 * sqrt(x) / x, rounded to the nearest whole number, where x represents the division number. A full breakdown of each division's buyin can be found on the information page of the league website.

1.3.2. Payouts

The League payout varies by division, with higher divisions rewarding higher payouts and vice versa. The entirety of the total division buyin is rewarded to the division winner within a week of the division championship. A full breakdown of each division's payout can be found on the information page of the league website.

1.4. Drafts

1.4.1. Dates

The League will decide on draft dates via member vote following the announcement of season membership. The draft will take place between the NFL preseason and regular season, usually during an afternoon of the Labor Day Weekend. All divisions will draft on the same day.

1.4.2. Order

The League will determine both the order of the draft and the division conferences randomly. The draws will take place on the day of the draft. 1.4.3. Coordination

The League will coordinate the draft via Discord, including the draft date vote, the draft order draw, and the division conference draw. Additionally, the draft will take place in a Discord voice channel, to allow all members to communicate during the procedings. In some instances, a live draft event may be scheduled as well.

1.5. Results

1.5.1. Rewards

The League will recognize division winners with a reward, based on the number of consecutive division wins. Single win streaks will receive a custom championship ring, double win streaks will receive a custom championship belt, and triple win streaks will receive a custom championship trophy. Streaks are reset after the third consecutive win. The league rewards are the cost and fulfillment responsibilities of the commissioner.

1.5.2. Punishments

The League will sanction division losers with a punishment, based on the number of consecutive division losses. Single loss streaks will be forced to attend a professional women's sports game with no phone, double loss streaks will be forced to spend 24 hours straight in a poorly reviewed restaurant, and triple loss streaks will be forced to register as a sex offender. Streaks are reset after the third consecutive loss. The women's sports game, poorly reviewed restaurant, and county of registration will be mutually agreed upon by the division and the commissioner.

1.6. Properties

1.6.1. Website

The League will maintain the web page, the web server, and the web domain throughout the entire year, including the offseason.

1.6.2. Discord

The League will maintain the Discord server throughout the entire year, including the offseason.

1.6.3. Twitter

The League will maintain the Twitter account throughout the entire year, including the offseason.

2. The Players

2.1. Ownership

2.1.1. Application

The Players will submit the league application every season, regardless of returning or incoming status. Once submitted, the Players will be requested for payment and invited to their team by the commissioner.

2.1.2. Priority

The Players will be selected for membership by the commissioner, with priority status given to returning players over incoming players. Returning players will be invited to a team in the same division as the previous season, unless they were promoted or relegated to a new division.

2.2. Trades

2.2.1. Processing

The Players will deliberate, agree upon, and submit trade requests and waiver acquisitions without intervention from the commissioner. All trades and acquisitions will be subject to the processing timeline, and the commissioner will not process a transaction manually under any circumstances.

2.3. Offences

2.3.1. Collusion

The Players will not collude with other members under any circumstances. Collusion will be subject to critical sanctions, as outlined below, in all instances. The commissioner reserves sole authority to determine whether collusion was evident between any number of members.

2.3.2. Exchange

The Players will not exchange goods or services as a part of a trade under any circumstances. Exchange will be subject to major sanctions, as outlined below, in all instances. The commissioner reserves sole authority to determine whether exchange was leveraged between any number of players.

2.3.3. Churning

The Players will not churn waiver targets under any circumstances. Churning will be subject to minor sanctions, as outlined below, in all instances. The commissioner reserves sole authority to determine whether churning was intentionally created by any number of players.

2.3.4. Abandonment

The Players will not abandon lineups without strategic motives under any circumstances. Abandonment will be subject to trivial sanctions, as outlined below, in all instances. The commissioner reserves sole authority to determine whether abandoment was unstrategically caused by any number of players.

2.3.5. Other

The Players will not act in bad faith or against the spirit of the league under any circumstances. These actions may realize in any number of forms, as determined by the commissioner. As such, sanctions for the player's offense will be at the discretion of the commissioner.

2.4. Sanctions

2.4.1. Trivial

The Players will be sanctioned with a written warning for an offense warranting a trivial sanction. The sanction will occur during the current matchup period, regardless of when the offense took place.

2.4.2. Minor

The Players will be sanctioned with an individual flex removal for an offense warranting a minor sanction. The sanction will occur during the current matchup period, regardless of when the offense took place.

2.4.3. Major

The Players will be sanctioned with an entire roster removal for an offense warranting a major sanction. The sanction will occur during the current matchup period, regardless of when the offense took place.

2.4.4. Critical

The Players will be sanctioned with a season expulsion for an offense warranting a critical sanction. The sanction will occur during the current matchup period, regardless of when the offense took place.

3. The Commissioner

3.1. Restrictions

3.1.1. Ownership

The Commissioner will not be allowed to actively manage a team in any division, despite retaining part ownership in every team. In the event that division expansion results in empty teams without managers, the commissioner will enable automanager to handle the teams in question.

3.1.2. Amendments

The Commissioner will not alter the league bylaws without advvance notice to the entire league. Most amendments will be handled by league vote, but the commissioner reserves the right to the final say on all updates to the bylaws.

3.2. Obligations

3.2.1. Administration

The Commissioner will administrate every division within the league, actively preventing players offenses and ensuring fair play throughout the season. The commissioner will not interfere with the league by any method not outlined in the league bylaws.

3.2.2. Moderation

The Commissioner will moderate the league Discord server to avoid breaches of the terms of service. If required, the commissioner may appoint select, long-time members to assist with moderation.

3.2.3. Bookkeeping

The Commissioner will be solely responsible for the upkeep of the league website and its associated league history, so as to preserve the data collected over time. The commissioner is also responsible for the costs of the league properties, outlined above.

3.2.4. Commentary

The Commissioner will be solely responsible for providing updates and season commentary via the league Twitter account.

3.3. Authorities

3.3.1. Tiebreakers

The Commissioner will be responsible for breaking any ties, during both the regular and postseasons. This responsbility only applies to ties that cannot be broken by the parameters established in the league settings.

3.3.2. Anomalies

The Commissioner will retain full authority over the settlement of any anomalies that take place during the season. This includes, but is not limited to, abnormal game outcomes or cancellations, vacancies caused by expulsion or member retirement, and offenses taking place outside the immediate scope of the league.
